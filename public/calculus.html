What Is Calculus? Basics You Should Know

Calculus is a branch of mathematics that focuses on change. It’s divided into two main areas: differential calculus and integral calculus.

1. Differential Calculus

This area is concerned with the concept of a derivative. It helps you understand how things change at a particular moment. For example, the speed of a car at a specific second is found using derivatives.

2. Integral Calculus

Integral calculus deals with accumulation and area. It helps you calculate things like the area under a curve or the total distance traveled by an object over time.

Real-Life Applications

  • Engineering and physics problems
  • Economics – calculating cost, revenue, and profit
  • Biology – modeling population growth

Basic Terms to Know

  • Function: A relationship between input and output
  • Limit: What a function approaches as input approaches a value
  • Derivative: Instantaneous rate of change
  • Integral: The total area under a curve

Calculus is essential for higher-level math and science. Once you understand its basics, many complex problems become manageable.
