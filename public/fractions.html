Understanding Fractions: A Beginner’s Guide

Fractions are a way to represent parts of a whole. A fraction is made up of two numbers — the numerator (top number) and the denominator (bottom number). For example, 3/4 means 3 parts out of 4 equal parts.

Types of Fractions

  • Proper Fraction: Numerator is less than the denominator (e.g., 2/5).
  • Improper Fraction: Numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator (e.g., 7/4).
  • Mixed Number: A whole number and a fraction together (e.g., 1 3/4).

How to Add Fractions

To add fractions with the same denominator:

1/4 + 2/4 = 3/4

To add fractions with different denominators:

1/3 + 1/6 → find LCM of 3 and 6 = 6
→ (2/6 + 1/6) = 3/6 = 1/2

Multiplying Fractions

2/3 × 3/4 = (2×3)/(3×4) = 6/12 = 1/2

Why Fractions Matter

Fractions are used in everyday life: recipes, measurements, budgeting, and more. Understanding fractions builds a foundation for learning decimals, percentages, and algebra.
