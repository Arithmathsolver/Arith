Top 10 Common Mistakes in Algebra and How to Fix Them

Learning algebra can be tricky, especially when common mistakes get in the way. Here are ten of the most frequent algebra errors and tips to avoid them.

1. Not Following the Order of Operations

Always follow PEMDAS: Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication/Division, Addition/Subtraction.

2. Misplacing Negative Signs

Be cautious with subtraction and negative numbers, especially when distributing.

3. Incorrect Distribution

Wrong: 2(x + 3) = 2x + 3
Right: 2(x + 3) = 2x + 6

4. Combining Unlike Terms

You can only combine terms with the same variable and power.

5. Forgetting to Apply Operations to Both Sides

Whatever you do to one side of the equation, do to the other side too.

6. Dividing Incorrectly Across an Equation

Only divide once you've properly isolated terms or parentheses.

7. Dropping Variables

Don't forget to carry variables through every step of simplification.

8. Assuming x² + x = x²

These are not like terms. They cannot be combined.

9. Ignoring Zero and One

Multiplying by 0 gives 0. Multiplying by 1 keeps the value unchanged. Use them smartly.

10. Skipping the Check

Always plug your solution back into the original equation to verify it works.

Practice makes perfect! Understanding and avoiding these mistakes will make your algebra skills stronger.
