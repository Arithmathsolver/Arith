Solving Linear Equations – Explained with Examples

A linear equation is an equation in which the highest power of the variable is 1. It has the form ax + b = c, where a, b, and c are constants.

Steps to Solve Linear Equations

  1. Combine like terms on each side of the equation.
  2. Move variables to one side and constants to the other.
  3. Divide or multiply to isolate the variable.

Example 1:

Solve: 2x + 3 = 11
→ Subtract 3: 2x = 8
→ Divide by 2: x = 4

Example 2:

Solve: 3x - 5 = 2x + 4
→ Subtract 2x from both sides: x - 5 = 4
→ Add 5 to both sides: x = 9

Why Linear Equations Matter

Linear equations are fundamental in algebra and appear in various real-life contexts such as budgeting, distance-speed-time problems, and business planning.
